### Question 2

The worksheet in the attached workbook contains Put and Call option delta, gamma, and price for several strikes. The underlying futures prices (E-MINI S&P 500 futures Dec 2023) and the "continuous" futures price series are also provided.

All options, and the corresponding futures, expire on **Dec 15th, 2023**.  
For details on the security definitions and CME exchange, go to:

A basic result in option price theory is that an option payoff should be replicable with a self-financing portfolio invested in the underlying security and in the risk-free investment. In a Black and Scholes world, the amount of this portfolio invested in the underlying security is given by the Black delta (the relevant model for options on futures is the Black model).

A corollary of this is that, e.g., a portfolio given by a call option bought and its delta sold in the underlying security should behave as an investment in the risk-free rate of an amount equivalent to the value of the call itself.

Using ML and DL, find a function of the underlying price, the underlying return standard deviation, the strike, and the time to maturity (and whatever else you may deem relevant) which best approximates such a delta.

Compare this empirically based function with the proper Black delta formula and point out similarities and differences.

For those of you who are not familiar with options, and, in particular, with options on futures, I suggest as a reference any edition of Hull and White, *Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives*.

**In particular:** remember that the futures tracks the forward, not the spot of the underlying; this has implications on option pricing.
